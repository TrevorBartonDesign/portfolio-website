Hi, I'm Trevor.

I’m a UX/UI Designer with a background in languages, literature, illustration, and graphic design. I enjoy meeting with users, researching solutions, and solving problems in creative ways. I believe people deserve designs that are pixel-perfect, pretty, and palatable. I’m a speedy, and iterative designer, and I am always seeking to maximize my efficiency by perfecting my workflow. I'm one of the rare designers who also enjoys coding, and I love learning more about this side of the creative process.

I spent the first half of my twenties living in Tokyo, Japan, and the second half in Bologna, Italy. My time spent overseas taught me to be flexible, and how to communicate ideas succinctly to colleagues and users from diverse backgrounds. This influences the way I approach my designs. In addition, my past experience as an illustrator and graphic designer has given me a keen eye for composition, color, and typography.

Aside from UX/UI Design, I'm an avid watercolorist and inker, taking inspiration from Yoshitaka Amano, Studio Ghibli, and Ralph Bakshi. I'm also an amateur composer and enjoy playing saxophone, guitar, and keyboard. I’m particularly fond of the “Light Music” and “Exotica” genres from the 50s and 60s.
